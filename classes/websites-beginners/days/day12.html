Coding Websites with HTML/CSS Workshop Curriculum Day 12

Note that from here on out the days are going to have much less material to cover. This is so that you, the teacher, can have a more open ended class sessions. If you feel that your students can handle more that what is listed here then feel free to move onto the next day. We do hope, however, that you give a good 45 minutes to the students to work on their sites.

Process

  • Define
    • Who - Who you are communicating to (Target Audience)?
    • What - What are you communicating/selling (Value Proposition)?
    • Why - Why do they need to pay attention (Pain Point)?
    • When - When is always “now” (Persuading Argument)
    • How - How can they “act now” on the “why” (Call to Action)?
  • Describe
  • Sketch idea
  • Create - Build in software
  • Test
    • Show your friends and invite feedback. Be honest with each other. Ask questions like:
    • Does this accurately communicate the full message?
    • Does this communicate why there even is a message?
    • Will the person the design is intended for (Target Audience) understand it?
    • Will the target audience know it is for them?

Fact Finding

  • (Design brief) What do we want to say / Who are we saying it to / Who is saying it
    • Clearly stated message
    • Are we trying to persuade? (sales)
    • Are we communicating information? (event)
    • Are we raising (brand) awareness?
  • (Design tone) Ideate company personality, reputation, perception -> search terms -> mood board -> color scheme and font choices
  • Create the design
  • The 4 basic food groups of design are…(CRAP)
    • Contrast
    • Repetition
    • Alignment
    • Proximity
